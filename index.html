Definition & Origin

Definition: CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a structured methodology for planning and executing data mining and data science projects. It provides a clear framework to transform business questions into actionable insights using data.


Origin: Developed in the late 1990s by a consortium of companies including SPSS, NCR, and Daimler AG, CRISP-DM was designed to provide a vendor-neutral, cross-industry standard for data mining processes. Its six-phase framework ensures projects are systematic, repeatable, and business-focused.
