What is CRISP-DM?

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a structured methodology guiding data science projects in six iterative phases.

CRISP-DM Phases

1. Business Understanding

Define project goals, requirements, and business constraints.

2. Data Understanding

Explore the dataset (EDA) and identify patterns, trends, and anomalies.

3. Data Preparation

Clean, transform, and prepare data; handle missing values and features.

4. Modeling

Select algorithms, train models, and tune parameters.

5. Evaluation

Assess model performance and ensure it meets business objectives.

6. Deployment

Deploy the model and present results via reports, dashboards, or systems.

Purpose & Applications

Purpose: CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) provides a structured and systematic framework for data mining projects. Its goal is to bridge the gap between business goals and data-driven solutions, ensuring that projects deliver actionable insights efficiently and effectively.


Applications:

  • Predicting customer behavior (e.g., churn, purchase likelihood).
  • Detecting fraud in banking and finance.
  • Optimizing marketing campaigns through segmentation and targeting.
  • Forecasting demand and inventory in retail.
  • Improving patient outcomes and resource allocation in healthcare.
  • Analyzing social media and text data for sentiment or trend analysis.
