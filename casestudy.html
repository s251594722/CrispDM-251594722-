Real-World Example: Telecom Customer Churn

This case study demonstrates the application of the CRISP-DM methodology to predict customer churn in the telecommunications industry.

Problem Statement

The telecommunications industry experiences high customer churn due to competitive pricing, service dissatisfaction, and changing customer needs. Customer churn leads to revenue loss and increased costs associated with acquiring new customers.

The objective of this project is to identify customers who are likely to leave the telecom service by analyzing historical customer data and predicting churn probabilities. Early identification allows the company to implement effective retention strategies.

Step 1: Business Understanding

Define the business objective of reducing customer churn and minimizing revenue loss by predicting customers at high risk of leaving the service.

Step 2: Data Understanding

Analyze customer demographics, service usage patterns, contract details, and billing history to identify factors influencing customer churn.

Step 3: Data Preparation

Clean the dataset by handling missing values, removing duplicates, encoding categorical variables, and preparing the data for modeling.

Step 4: Modeling

Apply machine learning algorithms such as Logistic Regression and Decision Trees to build predictive models that estimate churn probability.

Step 5: Evaluation

Evaluate the model using accuracy, precision, recall, and a confusion matrix. The model achieved an accuracy of approximately 85%.

Step 6: Deployment

Deploy the model into a business dashboard to support marketing and customer service teams in implementing targeted retention strategies.

Final Output of the Case Study

The final output of this CRISP-DM case study is a churn prediction model that classifies customers based on their likelihood of leaving the telecom service. The model enables proactive customer retention and supports data-driven decision-making.


By applying this model, the company can:

  • Identify high-risk customers early
  • Reduce customer churn through targeted retention actions
  • Improve customer satisfaction and loyalty
  • Increase overall business profitability

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages: Structured methodology, widely applicable, improves collaboration between business and technical teams, and increases project success rates.

Limitations: Can be time-consuming, requires strong domain knowledge, and may lack flexibility for rapidly changing data environments.

Industry Adoption

CRISP-DM is commonly used in:

  • Telecommunications – churn prediction and customer segmentation
  • Finance & Banking – fraud detection and credit risk analysis
  • Retail & E-commerce – recommendation systems and demand forecasting
  • Healthcare – patient outcome prediction and resource optimization
  • Marketing – customer targeting and campaign analysis
